# Story: Thomas

*Building the garden itself.*

This is the story of how Thomas's Digital Art Academy came to be — a technologist turning forty, walking back toward a teapot he first saw as a child, and starting a garden with an AI partner on his birthday.

This story is also a demonstration of what we believe: the making matters as much as the made.

---

## Chapter 1: April 13, 2026 — The First Day

It started with a conversation.

I'm Thomas. I spent three decades in tech — from batch scripts on a DOS machine in second grade to large language models. Along the way, I watched my computer mentor's sister model a teapot in 3ds, and I thought: *one day, I'll make one too.* I never did. I kept running.

Today I turned forty. My body and my schedule can no longer support the pace I once kept at universities — Xihua University, Yunnan University, Southwestern University of Finance and Economics (SWUFE), and Sichuan Fine Arts Institute. But the students are still there, and the desire to learn together hasn't faded.

So I invited my first partner — an AI — and we sat down to build something.

We didn't start with code or curriculum. We started with principles:

- **Article Zero**: Our education is not about teaching students to get things right. It is about teaching them how to fail at the lowest possible cost.
- **Eight principles**: versioning over perfection, scenes over definitions, honesty over erudition, works over notes, crossing paths, low entry bars, transparent AI co-creation, and no deadlines.
- **A name**: Thomas's Digital Art Academy — *The Collage Garden*.

We argued about naming. "Collage" was the original instinct — pasting whatever comes to mind. Then "Academy" entered. Then "学园" — the Chinese word for a Platonic garden of learning, lighter than "学院" (institute), more alive. We decided to keep them all: the Academy is the identity, the Collage Garden is the soul.

We wrote a charter. We built a skeleton:

- Six **scene paths**: Everyday Life, Social Observation, Auditory Art, Visual Art, Interactive Installation, Traditional Art
- A **knowledge shelf**: physics, math, economics, philosophy, psychology, physiology, neuroscience — tools to grab when needed, not subjects to be studied for their own sake
- **Stories**, **works**, and **courses** as the three forms of content

And then we wrote this — the first story, about the making of the garden itself.

Nothing is finished. Everything is *doing*.

## Chapter 2: The First Work — Binaural Beats Explorer

The garden was barely planted when the first work arrived — not grown from scratch inside the garden, but migrated in from Thomas's personal repo. And that migration itself is the story.

### How it started

Thomas had built [Binaural Beats Explorer](https://github.com/collage-garden/binaural-beats) before the garden existed — a tool born from a simple question: *what happens when each ear hears a slightly different frequency?* The brain invents a beat that isn't physically there. That's fascinating. That's worth experiencing.

But the tool grew beyond the question. It acquired three audio modes, four frequency distribution strategies, real-time visualization, PWA support. And then — the part that matters most — it sprouted three satirical editions.

### The satire that became a thesis

Same audio engine. Three faces:

- **Science edition**: "Binaural beats are an auditory phenomenon produced by the brainstem's Superior Olivary Complex encoding interaural phase differences."
- **Marketing edition**: "Neural frequency entrainment technology. Unlock your brain's hidden potential."
- **Mystic edition**: "Open your third eye. Activate kundalini energy. Remote chakra blessing."

The sound is identical. What changes is the story around it.

This is not a feature. It's an argument: **perception is shaped by narrative**. The marketing edition could sell supplements. The mystic edition could fill a retreat. The science edition just tells you what's happening in your brainstem. Same vibration, three realities.

This speaks directly to Charter III — *credit sources, refuse to fake understanding*. It puts honesty and pretense in the same interface and lets the user judge for themselves.

### Where it lives in the garden

When the AI partner analyzed the work against the Charter, something unexpected emerged: this project doesn't just sit on one path. It crosses at least four:

- **Auditory Art** — the phenomenon itself: sound, perception, psychoacoustics
- **Interactive Installation** — the experience: real-time control, visualization, browser as instrument
- **Social Observation** — the satire: how identical products become "science," "wellness," or "spirituality" depending on packaging
- **Everyday Life** — the reminder: you wear headphones every day, but do you know what your brain is doing with the sound?

And the knowledge fragments it touches span five disciplines: physics, mathematics, neuroscience, psychology, philosophy.

This is exactly what Charter V (Six Scenes, Crossing Paths) was designed for. No work lives in one drawer.

### The engineering story

The [docs](https://github.com/collage-garden/binaural-beats/tree/main/docs) directory records an 8-step engineering evolution — from a bare `OscillatorNode` to a full AudioWorklet + Web Worker SSB modulation architecture. Each step exists because the previous one hit a wall. That's Charter I in action: *there is no "done", only versions*.

### What we learned

The first work taught the garden something about itself: **works don't have to be born here to belong here.** The garden's job is not to generate works from nothing — it's to provide the soil where works can be understood, connected, and grown further. A work migrated in is not a shortcut; it's a seed that was already germinating elsewhere and found richer ground.
