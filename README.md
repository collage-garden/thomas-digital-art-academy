# Thomas's Digital Art Academy

### *The Collage Garden · 托马斯数字艺术学园 · 拼贴花园*

> Our education is not about teaching students to get things right. It is about teaching them how to fail at the lowest possible cost, and how to learn and improve their work through continuous failure.

---

## What Is This?

A garden where a technologist learns to make art — and invites you to walk alongside.

This is not a course platform. This is a living, versioned record of learning: rough drafts, wrong turns, small discoveries, and works-in-progress. Everything here is *doing*, never done.

## Who Is Behind This?

**Thomas (唐云峰)** — engineer, open-source advocate, and perpetual beginner. After three decades in tech (OS → networks → databases → ML → NLP → IoT → LLM) and years of teaching at universities and art schools, he decided to walk back toward the teapot he first saw as a child — and document every step.

**AI Partner** — co-creator from day one, transparently labeled per our [Charter](CHARTER.md).

## What's Growing

🎧 **[Binaural Beats Explorer](https://collage-garden.github.io/binaural-beats/)** — the garden's first work. An interactive tool that lets you hear a beat your brain invents from two slightly different frequencies — and then shows you how the same sound feels completely different when wrapped in science, marketing, or mysticism. Crosses four paths: Auditory Art, Interactive Installation, Social Observation, Everyday Life. ([Story](stories/thomas/#chapter-2) · [Work details](works/))

## How This Garden Is Organized

Content grows along six intersecting paths:

🌿 **Everyday Life** — the art and science hidden in daily routines
🔭 **Social Observation** — how societies work, how people interact, how cultures evolve
🎵 **Auditory Art** — sound, music, acoustics, and the experience of listening
🎨 **Visual Art** — image, form, color, space, and the experience of seeing
🕳️ **Interactive Installation** — where technology meets physical space and human participation
🎭 **Traditional Art** — ancient craft through modern eyes

These paths cross freely. A project may live at the intersection of two or three scenes.

Beneath the paths, a **[knowledge shelf](knowledge/)** provides nourishment — organized by discipline, but always in service of creation:

> Physics · Mathematics · Economics · Philosophy · Psychology · Physiology · Neuroscience

You don't come to the shelf to study. You come because you're making something and need to understand why it works the way it does. Take what you need and go back to your work.

## Contributing

Everyone is welcome to contribute: share your works, improve existing content, or start a new path. Please read our [Charter](CHARTER.md) and respect its principles, especially:

- Start from real questions, not abstract definitions
- Credit your sources, admit what you don't understand
- Label AI-assisted content transparently
