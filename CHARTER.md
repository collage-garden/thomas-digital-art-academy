# Charter of Thomas's Digital Art Academy

**The Collage Garden · 托马斯数字艺术学园 · 拼贴花园**

---

## Preamble

When I was in second grade, my computer mentor's sister was learning to model a teapot in 3ds. That was my first encounter with digital art. I spent the next three decades chasing technology — operating systems, networks, databases, machine learning, NLP, IoT, computer vision, large language models — always running, always further from that teapot.

Now, at forty, I want to walk back.

Not as an expert returning to teach, but as a learner who never really started. This academy is a garden where I plant what I learn, and anyone is welcome to walk through, pick things up, or plant something of their own.

This charter is the constitution of that garden.

---

## Article Zero — The Soul

**Our education is not about teaching students to get things right. It is about teaching them how to fail at the lowest possible cost, and how to learn and improve their work through continuous failure.**

An exhibited work is not finished — it is *doing*. If we never let learners attempt things they are uncertain about, how will they ever experience the power of the unknown?

This article takes precedence over all others.

---

## The Eight Principles

### I. There Is No "Done", Only Versions

Every piece of content in this garden is versioned. A first draft can be rough, even wrong. Being wrong is not shameful — our core promise is not correctness, but honest documentation. The Git history itself is a textbook: anyone can trace how an idea evolved from vague to clear.

### II. Start from a Real Question, Never from a Definition

No content shall begin with "Let's learn about Fourier transforms." It must begin with "Why does an erhu sound different from a violin?" — and the Fourier transform enters naturally in pursuit of the answer. Scene-first is not just an organizational method; it is a creative discipline.

### III. Credit Sources, Refuse to Fake Understanding

We will learn from and reference others' work extensively. All references must be properly attributed. When something is not understood, write "I don't understand this part yet." This is not weakness — it is our brand. Honesty matters more than erudition.

### IV. Works Over Notes

The ultimate output of this garden is not reading notes — it is works. A piece of audio, a generated image, an interactive webpage, a 3D model (yes, finally, the teapot). Every learning journey should end with "Here is what we made with what we learned." Notes document the process; works mark the milestones.

### V. Four Scenes, Crossing Paths

The four scenes — **Everyday Life**, **Auditory Art**, **Visual Art**, and **Social Observation** — are not parallel tracks. They are intersecting garden paths. A project about urban noise can belong to both Auditory Art and Social Observation. Content structure must support cross-referencing across scenes. No piece of knowledge is locked in one drawer.

### VI. Simple Enough for a Curious Freshman

Our learners range from art students to graduate researchers. The entry bar must be set for a curious first-year student with no programming background. Depth can extend infinitely, but the doorway must be low enough for anyone to walk through. Every topic begins by assuming the reader knows nothing.

### VII. AI Co-creation, Transparently Labeled

This academy is co-created with AI from day one. All AI-contributed content shall be transparently marked — not to draw boundaries, but because honesty is our first principle. Readers have the right to know. This is also a demonstration for the future of education: human-AI co-creation is not cheating, but it demands transparency.

### VIII. No Deadlines — The Garden Grows at Its Own Pace

This project shall not become a source of pressure. There are no deadlines, no update commitments. The garden grows at the maintainer's rhythm. Some seasons are lush; some are dormant. Both are natural.

## Foundational Settings

### Language

Primary language: **English**. When a topic reaches a complete state (which, per Principle I, is always provisional), multilingual versions will be created.

- **Code** (scripts, tools, configurations): [MIT License](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)

### Content Organization

Content is organized by **scenes** (the four garden paths), supported by **knowledge fragments** from various disciplines.

**Scenes** — the four paths through the garden:

| Scene | What it explores |
|---|---|
| **Everyday Life** | The art and science hidden in daily routines |
| **Auditory Art** | Sound, music, acoustics, and the experience of listening |
| **Visual Art** | Image, form, color, space, and the experience of seeing |
| **Social Observation** | How societies work, how people interact, how cultures evolve |

**Knowledge Fragments** — the toolkit, not the curriculum:

| Discipline | What it provides |
|---|---|
| **Physics** | Mechanics, acoustics, optics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism |
| **Mathematics** | Geometry, algebra, calculus, statistics, signals, information theory |
| **Economics** | Markets, behavioral economics, game theory, resource allocation |
| **Philosophy** | Aesthetics, epistemology, ethics, logic, phenomenology |
| **Psychology** | Perception, cognition, emotion, creativity, behavioral patterns |
| **Physiology** | Sensory systems, motor control, embodied experience |
| **Neuroscience** | Neural processing, auditory/visual cortex, plasticity, consciousness |

These knowledge fragments are organized systematically so they can be found when needed — but they exist to **serve the scenes**, not to dominate them. A student does not come to this garden to "study physics." A student comes to make something, discovers a need to understand vibration, finds what they need in the knowledge shelf, and returns to their work.

### Content Forms

- **Stories** — The creative process itself: creation journals, learning narratives, dead ends, sparks, and unexpected turns. Not every story becomes a work, but every work begins with a story. Stories are the roots; works are the fruit.
- **Knowledge notes** — Learning fragments, references, explorations
- **Scenario works** — Creative pieces born from scene-based learning
- **Courses** — Structured learning paths assembled from stories, notes, and works

### Roles

- **Maintainer**: Thomas (唐云峰) — learner, gardener, and keeper of the charter
- **Co-creator**: AI partner — co-creates content, challenges ideas, offers perspectives
- **Contributors**: Everyone — welcome to co-create, share works, and walk the garden paths
